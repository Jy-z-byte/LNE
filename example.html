London’s Nighttime Economy: Vitality, Social Equity, Safety and Risk

This project examines the spatial distribution of night economy activities in London, with a particular focus on social equity and urban safety. Specifically, it seeks to:

  • Understand how the intensity of night-time economic activities correlates with local wealth and accessibility.
  • Assess the safety conditions in these areas by analyzing factors such as surveillance coverage, police presence, and crime incidents. 

By integrating data-driven insights with interactive public engagement, this project aims to bring public’s attention to the urban safety and economic vitality in London's nightlife.

The data used in this project come from multiple sources, including:

  • Night Economy Infrastructure: cultural infrastructure POI from London Datastore.
  • Mobility & Accessibility: night bus operation situation from TfL, traffic routes and stops data from OSM.
  • Socioeconomic Indicators: Borough income levels from London Datastore.
  • Crime & Safety Data: metropolitan police night crime records from London Datastore, police station locations POI and CCTV coverage from OSM.

To develop the websites, a combination of tools and frameworks were used:

  • R & Excel: Data cleaning and statistical analysis.
  • Felt & Mapbox GL JS: Interactive mapping, including the 3D extrusion map and borough-level crime trends.
  • D3.js: Custom visualizations for dynamic data representation.
  • Menti & Ushahidi: Public participation features such as word clouds, voting mechanisms, and incident reporting.
  • Figma, VS Code & Github: Websites Building and Publishing.
