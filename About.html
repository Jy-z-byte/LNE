About This Project

This website is a project by NUS AY2024/2025 DEP5118 Group 6, with members including ZHANG Jingyi, LUO tianle, and LUO Geqi, under the guidance of Assistant Prof. Ahn Chaewon. It explores London's nighttime economy, focusing on vitality, social equity, and safety through interactive visualizations and public participation.

As this is a student project, there may be limitations—your feedback is highly appreciated! Please click the " Feedback" button in the top right corner or email us at e1350830@u.nus.edu or e1349780@u.nus.edu or e1351007@u.nus.edu.

User Manual
Management Plan & Data Updates

This website is designed for both data exploration and public participation. As Citizen user, you can:

  • Interact with visual maps : Explore crime and economic trends, examine the spatial patterns in the visualized data.
  • Provide insights on nighttime economy : Share experiences and safety concerns to support better planning.
  • Engage with participatory elements : Contribute to a word cloud, vote on your favorite nightlife boroughs, or report crime incidents in nightlife areas.
  • As a student project, this website is not under long-term maintenance, but we have considered ways to keep the data relevant and explore future improvements.
  • Data Updates: The crime and economic data are based on publicly available sources and can be manually updated on a yearly basis when new datasets are released. While we cannot guarantee continuous updates, we hope the structured workflow allows future contributors to build on our work.
  • Project Maintenance: Since this is an academic project, there is no dedicated team for long-term maintenance. However, if a research group or institution is interested in adopting or expanding it, integration with APIs or more automated processes could be explored.
  • Future Possibilities: Potential future improvements include refining participatory elements, optimizing the interface for better usability, expanding the dataset coverage, and even extending similar research to more cities. While these enhancements are beyond our current scope, they remain open directions for further development.

Research Data

The data used in this project come from multiple sources, including:

  • Night Economy Infrastructure: cultural infrastructure POI from London Datastore.
  • Mobility & Accessibility: night bus operation situation from TfL, traffic routes and stops data from OSM.
  • Socioeconomic Indicators: Borough income levels from London Datastore.
  • Crime & Safety Data: metropolitan police night crime records from London Datastore, police station locations POI and CCTV coverage from OSM.
Research Methodology

To develop the websites, a combination of tools and frameworks were used:

  • R & Excel: Data cleaning and statistical analysis.
  • Felt & Mapbox GL JS: Interactive mapping, including the 3D extrusion map and borough-level crime trends.
  • D3.js: Custom visualizations for dynamic data representation.
  • Menti & Ushahidi: Public participation features such as word clouds, voting mechanisms, and incident reporting.
  • Figma, VS Code & Github: Websites Building and Publishing.
